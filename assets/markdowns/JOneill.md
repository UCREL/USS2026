## 🎤 Talk

### AI for Everyday Lives

> Rapid advances in frontier models and generative AI promise to revolutionise how we interact with technology, with information, and as a consequence transform workplaces globally. But whose lives are actually being transformed? For many communities, particularly across the Global South, the reality lags far behind the promise. This  disconnect risks deepening the digital divide and entrenching systemic inequalities. In this talk, I argue for a different path: one that centers multilingual and multicultural AI, leverages small and locally deployable models, and embraces edge computing. Ultimately, I argue that equitable AI is not just a technical challenge — it requires rethinking how we center people, contexts, and lived experience in the design and deployment of AI systems.

## 👤 About

Dr Jacki O'Neill is a computer scientist and founding director of Microsoft Research Africa, Nairobi, where she has built a globally recognised centre for equitable, human-centred AI. Her work focuses on ensuring AI systems are linguistically and culturally appropriate, particularly in health, agriculture, and the future of work across Africa. She received the ACM SIGCHI Societal Impact Award in 2026, and her research has centred the experiences of those most affected by technology — from gig workers and auto-rickshaw drivers to front-line workers and small businesses. Her work spans Africa, Asia, Europe and the USA and has influenced both research and real-world products. 

Dr O'Neill combines deep field research with high-stakes technology design. She co-led a landmark AI and Future of Work white paper with the African Union and Oxford University, and led the creation of Paza — including ASR models, playbooks, and PazaBench, the first speech-recognition leaderboard for African low-resource languages. With more than 50 peer-reviewed publications and 13 patents, she is an influential voice in Human-Computer Interaction and a leading advocate for building AI that works for everyone. 

Outside of work, her passions include running, hiking and camping.
